Graduate Coursework

Selected coursework from the M.Sc. in Economics at CAEN/UFC, emphasizing mathematical methods, economic theory, modern macroeconomic modeling, and econometric training.

Mathematical Economics

Graduate mathematical methods for optimization, dynamics, and theoretical economics.

Foundations and analysis

Proofs; induction; metric spaces; vector spaces; sequences; open, closed, bounded, and compact sets.

Multivariable calculus and optimization

Continuity; inverse functions; Weierstrass theorem; Jacobians; Hessians; concavity and convexity; implicit function theorem; Lagrange and Kuhn-Tucker methods.

Integration and dynamics

Multiple integration; change of variables; fixed point theorems; Tarski and Kakutani; differential equations; difference equations; Picard and Peano theorems.

Main references: Almeida; Lima; Simon and Blume; Chiang and Wainwright; de la Fuente.

Microeconomics

Graduate microeconomic theory covering consumer and producer behavior, market equilibrium, strategic interaction, and information economics.

Consumer, firm, and markets

Consumer theory; producer theory; market equilibrium; welfare; general equilibrium; choice under uncertainty.

Game theory

Static and dynamic games; Nash equilibrium; subgame perfection; repeated games; mixed strategies; Bayesian games.

Information and mechanisms

Principal-agent models; contracts; moral hazard; adverse selection; screening; signaling; auctions.

Main references: Jehle and Reny; Mas-Colell, Whinston and Green; Gibbons.

Macroeconomics

Graduate macroeconomic theory combining recursive methods, growth theory, business-cycle models, monetary macroeconomics, and heterogeneous-agent frameworks.

Recursive methods and dynamic programming

Finite- and infinite-horizon dynamic programming; Bellman equations; contraction mapping; recursive competitive equilibrium; consumption and asset-pricing applications.

Growth and long-run development

Solow model; productivity differences; neoclassical growth; welfare analysis; endogenous growth; human capital; input-variety models; Schumpeterian growth.

Business cycles and monetary models

RBC models; New Classical models; New Keynesian models; nominal rigidities; monetary policy; dynamic general equilibrium.

Heterogeneous-agent macroeconomics

Incomplete markets; idiosyncratic risk; heterogeneous-agent models; HANK-type frameworks; distributional effects of aggregate shocks and policy.

Main references: Acemoglu; Barro and Sala-i-Martin; Romer; Ljungqvist and Sargent; Stokey, Lucas and Prescott.

Econometrics

Econometric theory and applied methods for regression, causal interpretation, robust inference, and empirical research design.

Regression foundations

Conditional expectation functions; best linear prediction; omitted variable bias; identification; causal interpretation; OLS algebra; projection matrices; Frisch-Waugh-Lovell theorem.

Inference and asymptotics

Finite-sample OLS; normal regression; maximum likelihood; WLLN; CLT; Slutsky theorem; delta method; Wald, F, Hausman, and score tests.

Robust and computational inference

Heteroskedasticity-consistent inference; leverage diagnostics; leave-one-out regression; Monte Carlo simulation; jackknife; bootstrap; cluster-aware resampling.

Applied econometric models

Instrumental variables; weak instruments; panel data; simultaneous equations; limited dependent variables; count data; censored and truncated regression; model selection.

Main references: Greene; Wooldridge; Davidson and MacKinnon; Hayashi.
