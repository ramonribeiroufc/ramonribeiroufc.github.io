Manuscripts Under Review

Place-Based Development Credit and Productive Transformation in Lagging Regions: Evidence from Brazil’s Northeast Development Fund

Solo-authored manuscript · Under review at Regional Studies

Regional development Development finance Shift-share designs Brazil
This paper studies whether place-based development credit transforms lagging local economies or mainly expands their scale. I examine Brazil’s Northeast Development Fund (FNE), a large regional credit program targeted to the SUDENE area, using a municipality-year panel and a shift-share exposure measure based on predetermined sectoral value-added shares and external sector-year FNE credit shocks. The results show that predicted exposure increases formal employment, payroll, establishments, private and industrial value added, and GDP per capita, with particularly strong extensive-margin effects in low-capacity municipalities. The central finding is that productive conversion is capacity-dependent: value added per worker rises in higher-capacity municipalities, while low-capacity municipalities experience expansion without proportional productivity gains. The paper argues that regional development credit can be effective at formalization and scale, but that absorptive capacity shapes whether credit-induced expansion becomes productive transformation.

Annual Municipal Inequality in Brazil Between Censuses: A Grouped Administrative Gini, 2012–2023

Solo-authored manuscript · Under review at Review of Income and Wealth

Inequality Administrative data Municipal Brazil Measurement
This paper develops an annual municipal inequality indicator for Brazil from 2012 to 2023 using a grouped-distribution approach built from public administrative records, official population statistics, and municipal economic diagnostics. Rather than predicting inequality from municipal covariates, the paper reconstructs a municipality-year income distribution from ordered grouped cells and computes a Lorenz-based grouped Gini. The lower tail is anchored in Cadastro Único, formal labor income is organized through RAIS, pension income is projected onto the same income grid, and uncovered resources are incorporated through an explicit residual-income rule. The raw index is calibrated once to the 2010 municipal benchmark. The resulting series documents persistent municipal inequality, stable regional ordering, and strong spatial continuity in the geography of change, while robustness exercises show that alternative assumptions affect levels more than temporal and territorial patterns.

Who Bears the Burden of Monetary Tightening? Credit Default Responses Across Income Groups and Firm Sizes in Brazil

With Pedro Igor Araújo de Oliveira and Victor Lira Stilita · Under review at International Economics and Economic Policy

Monetary policy Credit default Distributional effects Firm size
This paper examines heterogeneous monetary transmission to credit default in Brazil using monthly Central Bank data from 2012 to 2024 disaggregated by income bracket, firm size, and state. The analysis documents that interest-rate movements predict subsequent default deterioration unevenly across borrowers. For households, the strongest responses are concentrated in the broader central segment of the income distribution, especially lower-middle and middle-income borrowers, with effects becoming visible after several months. For firms, small businesses show the clearest and strongest responses, while large firms remain more insulated. Additional mechanism exercises based on occupational, sectoral, and product-level splits suggest that firm-side heterogeneity is closely related to credit segmentation and exposure to free and short-term credit. The paper contributes reduced-form evidence on the distributional burden of monetary tightening in an emerging market credit system.

Selected Working Papers

When Water Arrives: Large-Scale Infrastructure and Drought Resilience in the Brazilian Semi-Arid

With Christiano Modesto Penna · Work in progress · Target journal: World Development

Water infrastructure Climate adaptation Drought resilience Human capital
This paper studies whether Brazil’s São Francisco River Integration Project weakened the drought-damage function faced by municipalities in the Brazilian semi-arid. The empirical design compares immediate and later beneficiaries within the official project universe, exploiting staged operational access after 2017 and interacting drought intensity with active exposure and historical drought vulnerability. The analysis follows drought transmission across three theory-based margins: agricultural livelihoods, severe child-health outcomes, and upper-secondary schooling. Early exposure attenuated drought-related losses in agricultural production value, reduced severe child-health outcomes linked to infectious and parasitic mortality and dehydration hospitalizations, and lowered upper-secondary dropout in drought-exposed years. The paper argues that the development value of large water infrastructure is better captured by resilience during adverse rainfall states than by average growth effects alone.

Port-Led Industrialization and Local Economic Development: Evidence from the Pecém Industrial and Port Complex in Brazil

With Roberto Tatiwa Ferreira · Selected working paper

Port infrastructure Industrialization Generalized synthetic control Regional development
This paper estimates the local effects of the Pecém Industrial and Port Complex, a major port-industrial infrastructure project implemented in Northeast Brazil to attract industry and reshape the productive structure of a lagging region. We use a generalized synthetic control design to construct counterfactual trajectories for the two directly treated municipalities, Caucaia and São Gonçalo do Amarante, using the remaining municipalities of Ceará as donor units. The baseline window isolates the initial port-infrastructure phase before the Export Processing Zone began operating. The results indicate large positive effects on GDP per capita and industrial gross value added per capita, with the industrial response becoming stronger after an investment-maturation period. Robustness checks using alternative treatment dates, donor pools, and placebo diagnostics support the interpretation that Pecém generated a port-led industrialization process rather than only a broad short-run local-demand shock.

A Thorough Analysis of the Brazilian National Inclusion Policy for Students with Disability

With Virna Vidal Menezes and Ângelo dos Santos · Selected working paper

Education policy Disability inclusion School Census Brazil
This project analyzes Brazil’s National Policy of Special Education, implemented after 2008 to promote the inclusion of students with disabilities in regular public schools and expand specialized support through resource rooms. The analysis uses Brazilian School Census microdata from 2007 to 2017 to document changes in access, school composition, specialized infrastructure, and the enrollment trajectories of students with disabilities. A central distinction is between the inclusion mandate, which affected all schools, and the uneven expansion of Special Resource Classrooms, which provided schools with additional infrastructure and pedagogical support. The project first characterizes the national diffusion of inclusive education across municipalities, schools, and students, and then motivates subsequent causal analysis of the effects of inclusion and specialized support on students with disabilities and their peers.
